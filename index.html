What is MUSHT?

Make Underutilized Spuds Have Taste. If 100 % of every potato Earth grows in a year were extruded into one perfectly straight french fry, 0.75 cm × 0.75 cm square, it would run ~3.84 billion miles — past Pluto's average orbit (41 AU), 154,000× around the equator, a line a photon needs 5.7 hours to cross. Only ~6 % of the crop actually becomes fries, so the real fry line is ~0.23 billion miles — the gap is the story.

MUSHT — Make Underutilized Spuds Have Taste

The hero number: miles of french fry if the whole crop went straight to the fryer. Computed on raw-potato volume (ρ ≈ 1.08 g/cm³), 0.75 cm square cut.

The Fun Number

Extrude all 375 Mt of potato into one perfectly straight 0.75 cm fry and it stretches 3.84 billion miles — 41 AU, past Pluto. But only ~22.3 Mt (5.9 %) actually becomes fries, so the real fry line is 0.23 billion miles. Only about 1 potato in 17 ends up a fry.
