# Processing (Food) — Capsule

**Fate share:** ~14 % of production ≈ **52.5 Mt/yr** · Confidence 🟡

## What this captures
Potatoes entering food factories, split into four Tier-2 streams. Excludes
industrial (non-food) starch/alcohol, which is a separate Tier-1 line.

## Tier-2 split (of the 52.5 Mt processed)

| Category | Share of processing | Mt/yr | Conf |
|----------|--------------------|-------|------|
| Frozen (fries/wedges/tots) | 50 % | 26.2 | 🔴 |
| Crisps / chips | 24 % | 12.6 | 🔴 |
| Dehydrated (flakes → mash) | 14 % | 7.3 | 🔴 |
| Canned / other | 12 % | 6.3 | 🔴 |

→ **Frozen fries** get their own capsule: [frozen_fries](frozen_fries/REPORT.md).

## Findings
- Global processing was ~**10 % of production in 2013** and has been rising
  ([review][rev]).
- Enormous regional spread: the **Netherlands and US process 55–60 %** of their
  crop, **China ~15 %**, **India ~7 %** ([review][rev]). Because China + India are
  the two largest producers (~40 % of world output) and process little, the
  **global weighted share stays modest (~14 %)** even as absolute processed
  volume grows fast.
- Frozen (fries-led) is the largest processed category worldwide, dominated by
  **McCain, Lamb Weston and J.R. Simplot** ([Foods review][mdpi]).
