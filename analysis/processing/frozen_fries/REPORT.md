# Frozen Fries — Capsule (the MUSHT engine)

**Fries stream:** ~**29.8 Mt/yr** of potatoes ≈ **7.9 % of all production** ·
Confidence 🟡

## Derivation (Run 2)
```
Processing 17% × Frozen 55% × fries-within-frozen 85%  = 7.95% of production
0.0795 × 375 Mt ≈ 29.8 Mt of potato going into frozen fries
```
Sanity check: USDA reports the US alone used ~176 M cwt (~8.0 Mt) of raw potato
for frozen products in 2022; ~85 % fries ≈ 6.8 Mt, so the US is ~23 % of a ~30 Mt
global fries stream — consistent with Europe (Belgium/NL) being the largest
frozen-fry producing/exporting region.

**Cut geometry → its own [deep dive](../../deep-dives/fry-cuts-by-chain/REPORT.md):**
straight 72 % · crinkle 8 % · wedge 6 % · waffle 5 % · tots 5 % · **curly 4 %
(≈ 12 M miles/yr, mostly Arby's).**

## Findings
- Frozen fries are the flagship processed potato product; **McCain, Lamb Weston
  and J.R. Simplot** control ~**80 % of the US frozen-fry market** and lead
  globally ([majors][maj]).
- Scale check: **Simplot alone uses ~3 Mt of potatoes** for its products
  ([majors][maj]) — consistent with a ~22 Mt global fries stream once all makers
  and regions are summed.
- Europe is the largest frozen-fry producing/exporting region; Belgium and the
  Netherlands are major exporters, Asia a fast-growing importer ([market][opr]).

## Why it matters — Real MUSHT
This 29.8 Mt is the **only** part of the harvest that literally becomes fries, so
it drives **Real MUSHT ≈ 0.30 billion miles** (vs. the idealized 3.84 billion if
*all* spuds were fries). The ~3.5-billion-mile gap is the headline: **only ~1
potato in 13 becomes a fry.**

## Run-4: processor corroboration & finished-fry weight

The **big three** corroborate the ~30 Mt raw scale:

| Rank | Processor | Signal |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | **McCain Foods** | makes **~1 in 4 fries worldwide**; ~$9 B sales; world's largest |
| 2 | **Lamb Weston** | world #2; largest in North America (~$6 B+) |
| 3 | **J.R. Simplot** | ~3 Mt potatoes/yr; key McDonald's supplier |

**Raw vs finished:** the 29.8 Mt is **raw potato** (the MUSHT basis). At a ~50 %
finished-weight yield (peel/trim/moisture), that is **~14.9 Mt of finished
fries**. McCain's "1 in 4" of ~15 Mt finished ⇒ a few Mt of finished product for
McCain alone — the right order of magnitude, so raw ≈ 30 Mt holds up.
