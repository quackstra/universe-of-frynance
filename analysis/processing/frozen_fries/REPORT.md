# Frozen Fries — Capsule (the MUSHT engine)

**Fries stream:** ~**22.3 Mt/yr** of potatoes ≈ **5.9 % of all production** ·
Confidence 🔴

## Derivation
```
Processing 14% × Frozen 50% × fries-within-frozen 85%  = 5.95% of production
0.0595 × 375 Mt ≈ 22.3 Mt of potato going into frozen fries
```

## Findings
- Frozen fries are the flagship processed potato product; **McCain, Lamb Weston
  and J.R. Simplot** control ~**80 % of the US frozen-fry market** and lead
  globally ([majors][maj]).
- Scale check: **Simplot alone uses ~3 Mt of potatoes** for its products
  ([majors][maj]) — consistent with a ~22 Mt global fries stream once all makers
  and regions are summed.
- Europe is the largest frozen-fry producing/exporting region; Belgium and the
  Netherlands are major exporters, Asia a fast-growing importer ([market][opr]).

## Why it matters — Real MUSHT
This 22.3 Mt is the **only** part of the harvest that literally becomes fries, so
it drives **Real MUSHT ≈ 0.23 billion miles** (vs. the idealized 3.84 billion if
*all* spuds were fries). The ~3.6-billion-mile gap is the headline: **only ~1
potato in 17 becomes a fry.**
