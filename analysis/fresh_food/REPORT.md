# Fresh / Table Food — Capsule

**Fate share:** ~49 % of production ≈ **183.8 Mt/yr** · Confidence 🟡

> **Run-2 update:** trimmed from 52 % → 49 % after the country-weighted
> processing rebuild lifted processing to 17 %. Fresh is still the largest fate,
> concentrated in China, India and the rest of the world.

## What this captures
Whole potatoes sold for cooking at home or in foodservice (boiled, baked,
roasted, mashed-from-scratch) — *not* factory-processed products, which are the
[Processing](../processing/REPORT.md) capsule.

## Findings
- Potatoes are the **world's third most important food crop** for human
  consumption after wheat and rice, with ~**1.3 billion people** eating them as a
  staple (>50 kg/person/yr in parts of India and China) ([CIP][cip]).
- Fresh consumption is the single largest fate: CIP puts fresh at roughly
  **two-thirds of the harvest**; reconciled against processing/feed/seed/waste in
  a full mass balance, fresh table use lands near **52 %**, with total *human
  food* (fresh + processed) ≈ two-thirds ([CIP][cip]).
- The developing world now grows **more than half** of all potatoes and consumes
  most of it fresh, while developed markets shift toward processed forms ([CIP][cip]).

## Method / basis
Fresh-weight. Derived as the residual of the FAO Food Balance Sheet food line
after processing is separated out, anchored to CIP's "two-thirds fresh"
statement. See `workings/`. This is the largest 🟡 line and the top Run-2 target
for a country-weighted FBS rebuild.

## Confidence & gaps
🟡 — the fresh/processing boundary is the main uncertainty; a country-weighted
FBS pull (China + India + EU + US cover >60 % of production) would tighten it.

[cip]: https://cipotato.org/potato/potato-facts-and-figures/
