# Waste & Loss — Capsule (the Conscience Number)

**Two distinct figures — do not conflate:**

| Figure | Value | Role |
|--------|-------|------|
| FBS accounted losses (Tier-1 line) | ~8 % ≈ 30 Mt | Part of the 100 % mass balance |
| **Full-chain Food Loss & Waste (overlay)** | **~45 % ≈ 169 Mt** | The Conscience Number — *not* summed into Tier-1 |

Confidence 🟡 (overlay) / 🔴 (FBS line).

## Why two numbers
The FAO Food Balance Sheet "losses" line captures a narrow slice of post-harvest
loss and is one of the six Tier-1 fates that partition production. **Food Loss &
Waste (FLW)** is a *cross-cutting overlay* measured field→consumer; it spans
several fates (a wasted table potato was "fresh food"; a rejected fry offcut was
"processing"). Summing FLW into Tier-1 would double-count — so the overlay is
reported separately. This is the project's core anti-double-count rule.

## Findings
- **Roots & tubers have the highest food-loss rate of any commodity group
  (>25 %)**, with cassava and potato the main drivers ([FAO GFLW][fao]).
- Regional range for roots & tubers: **33 %** (N. Africa / W & C Asia) to **60 %**
  (N. America & Oceania) ([FAO GFLW][fao]).
- Potatoes are frequently described as **"reduced by half from field to fork"**
  ([TableDebates][td]) — hence the ~45 % overlay headline (range 25–60 %).
- Fresh vs. processed asymmetry: ~**30 %** of *fresh* potato losses are disposed
  outright, but only **4–5 %** of *processing* losses — processing is far more
  efficient at using the whole tuber ([Frontiers review][fr]).

## Confidence & gaps
🟡 on the ~45 % overlay (wide regional spread); 🔴 on the 8 % FBS line pending a
direct FBS pull. Run-2: stage-resolved loss (field / storage / processing /
retail / consumer) and a fresh-vs-processed breakdown.
